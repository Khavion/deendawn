# Reciter permission — ready-to-send email

**What this is:** the one real content gap is Quran recitation audio. As the
research confirmed, almost no well-known reciter's recordings are free to ship,
so the clean path is written permission. Below is an email you can send to a
reciter, a reciter's institution/publisher, or a masjid whose imam records.
Fill the three [BRACKETS], send, and forward me whatever they reply — a "yes"
with any terms is enough for me to wire their audio in.

**Who to send it to (any of these):**

- A local imam/qārī whose recitation you like and who might be happy to have it
  in a free, no-ads app.
- A reciter's official website/management (look for a "contact" or "licensing"
  address).
- A Quran-teaching institution that owns recordings.

**Tip:** start with someone you or your community has a connection to — a
personal "yes" is far more likely than a cold email to a famous reciter.

---

## The email

**Subject:** Permission request — your recitation in a free, non-commercial Quran app

Assalamu alaikum [RECITER OR ORGANISATION NAME],

My name is [YOUR NAME], and I'm building a free Islamic app called DeenDawn
(prayer times, qibla, the Quran, and more). It has no ads, no user accounts,
and does not track anyone — it is a sadaqah-jariyah project, not a business.

I would be honoured to include your recitation of the Holy Quran in the app so
that users can listen while they read. I'm writing to ask your permission.

Specifically, I would like to:

- Host your audio recordings on my own private storage and stream them inside
  the app (they would not be downloadable or resold);
- Display a clear, permanent credit to you as the reciter, in whatever wording
  you prefer;
- Begin with a few chapters (surahs) if you'd like to start small, and add the
  full Quran once you're comfortable.

To be completely clear about the app:

- It is and always will be free, with no advertising.
- It does include an optional "tip" button that lets users support the app's
  development costs (it is never framed as charity or zakat) — I mention this
  for full honesty, though the app makes no profit.
- Your recordings would only ever be used to help people listen to the Quran;
  they would never be modified or used for anything else.

If you're willing, could you let me know in writing (even a short email reply is
fine) that you permit this use, along with any conditions you'd like — how you
want to be credited, which recordings I may use, and anything I should avoid?
If you'd prefer a formal agreement, I'm glad to sign whatever you send.

JazakAllahu khayran for considering this, and for your service to the Quran.

Wassalam,
[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR EMAIL]
DeenDawn

---

## After they reply

Forward me their answer. If it's a yes, tell me:

1. Where the audio files are (a link, a folder, or they email them to you), and
2. Exactly how they want to be credited.

I'll verify the files, put them on the app's storage, and switch the listening
feature on — it's already fully built and just waiting for cleared audio.

**Note on framing (kept honest):** the email mentions the optional tip button on
purpose. If a reciter only permits _strictly non-commercial_ use, the tip jar
could be argued to make the app commercial-adjacent — so it's better they know
up front and say yes with full information than for it to become an issue later.
